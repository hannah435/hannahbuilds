01About

The short version

These days I call myself an AI marketing technologist, the shortest honest way to say I can market the thing and build the thing.

Six years deep in Web3 now, mostly across one multi-brand ecosystem (BitAngels, Tokenize Conference, Transform Group), running the content and quietly building the AI tools that produce it.

But it didn't start there. My career didn't arrive fully formed; it emerged, one layer building on the last. I'd rather make a thing and learn by breaking it than read about it forever.

02Journey

How I got here

  1. First, community

    It started with people, not whitepapers

    My first real work in tech was growing communities across blockchain ecosystems, ICP initiatives, Omnity, Bitcoin Runes, and the usual sprawl of Telegram groups, Discord servers, and Twitter Spaces. That's where I learned the thing that still shapes everything: people don't adopt technology because of the whitepaper. They adopt it because of each other.

  2. Then, marketing

    Make the complicated thing simple

    Running communities showed me what made people get it, and what made their eyes glaze over. So I started making the content myself: threads, campaigns, contests, and brand narratives around Bitcoin, Runes, and cross-chain infrastructure. The rule never changed: take the complicated thing, make it simple, sound like a human.

  3. Then, business development

    Seeing the gaps

    Once you understand a space well enough to market it, you start seeing the gaps: the partnership that should exist, the bridge that's missing, the audience nobody's reaching. I spent a stretch connecting those dots.

  4. Then, building

    I stopped talking about it and built

    My first real builds were automations and chatbots, n8n and Zapier workflows for Omnity, content-intake systems, and an AI chatbot system for a healthcare clinic in Australia that took the manual response load off the staff. That clinic work pulled my building out of crypto and into the real world.

    Then it kept growing. I designed and built whole sites from scratch (icphubsg.com in Squarespace and custom CSS), then moved into production React and Supabase, shipping tools end to end like Spotlight, a speaker platform I built as a Sessionize alternative. Somewhere in there the builder stopped being a side character.

  5. Now, AI marketing technologist

    The two halves became one job

    These days the marketing and the building aren't separate anymore, they're the same job. I build the tools that make the content: Skein, an internal toolkit of 15 SEO tools on a single Cloudflare + Supabase + Claude engine; AI content systems running social across the brands; and the n8n automations holding it all together. Market the thing, then build the thing that markets the thing.
